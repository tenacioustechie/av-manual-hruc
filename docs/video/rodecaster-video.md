# RodeCaster Video

The **RodeCaster Video** is the box that runs the livestream. It takes the two
cameras and the livestream audio, lets you choose which camera is shown, and
sends the finished video to **YouTube**.

This page explains its main controls, then how to **start** and **stop** the
livestream.

---

## What the main parts do

### The screen
The RodeCaster Video has a **touchscreen** showing previews of the cameras and
the current output (what viewers see).

### The source / scene buttons
Buttons (and matching StreamDeck buttons) let you choose **which camera** is on
the livestream — Camera 1, Camera 2, or a prepared scene.

### The Stream button
This **starts and stops** sending the video to YouTube. When live, it shows a
**streaming** indicator.

### The audio meters
Small bars that move with the sound. They confirm the **livestream audio**
(from the QU-5D) is arriving.

---

## How to start the livestream

Do this 2–3 minutes before the 9:30am service.

1. Make sure the **cameras** are on and showing a picture on the RodeCaster
   screen.
2. Make sure the **audio meters** move when someone speaks into a microphone.
3. Press the **Start Stream** button on the **StreamDeck** (recommended), or
   the **Stream** button on the RodeCaster Video.
4. Wait for the **streaming indicator** to confirm you are live.
5. Open **YouTube** on a phone or the PC to confirm the stream appears with
   picture and sound.

!!! tip "Use the StreamDeck"
    The easiest and most reliable way to start the stream is the **Start
    Stream** button on the StreamDeck — it runs the correct steps for you.
    See [StreamDeck Buttons](../presentation/streamdeck-buttons.md).

!!! warning "Check audio before you go live"
    The most common livestream complaint is "no sound online". Always confirm
    the **audio meters are moving** before you start. See
    [Livestream Mix](../audio/livestream-mix.md).

---

## How to switch cameras during the stream

1. Press the **Camera 1** or **Camera 2** button (on the StreamDeck or
   RodeCaster).
2. The livestream picture changes to that camera.

➡️ Full detail: [Camera Operation](camera-operation.md).

---

## How to stop the livestream

At the end of the service:

1. Keep streaming for a minute or two after the final words.
2. Press the **Stop Stream** button on the **StreamDeck** (or the **Stream**
   button on the RodeCaster).
3. Confirm the streaming indicator has switched **off**.

!!! warning "Always stop the stream before shutting down"
    Do not power equipment off while still live — viewers see an abrupt cut
    and YouTube may flag the broadcast. Stop the stream first. See
    [Sunday Shutdown](../quick-start/sunday-shutdown.md).

## If the stream won't start or drops out

| Symptom | First thing to check | Page |
|---------|---------------------|------|
| Stream won't start | Cameras showing a picture? Internet connected? | [No Camera Video](../troubleshooting/no-camera-video.md) |
| Picture but no sound online | Audio meters moving? Livestream matrix up? | [No Livestream Audio](../troubleshooting/no-livestream-audio.md) |
| Black / frozen picture online | Camera source selected? Camera powered? | [No Camera Video](../troubleshooting/no-camera-video.md) |
| Stream keeps disconnecting | Internet connection | [Network Overview](../system-design/network-overview.md) |

!!! note "The stream key lives in the RodeCaster"
    The RodeCaster Video is already set up with the church's YouTube stream
    settings. You should not need to enter a stream key. If YouTube reports
    the wrong account or channel, contact Mills IT — do not change the
    settings yourself.
