# Cleaning

Keeping the equipment clean helps it last and keeps it hygienic — especially
microphones that many people use. This page gives simple, safe cleaning
guidance. When in doubt, **do less**, and ask Mills IT.

!!! danger "Power and liquids don't mix"
    Never spray liquid directly onto any equipment. Never clean powered
    equipment with a wet cloth. Switch off and use only a **lightly damp** or
    dry cloth.

---

## General rules

- Use a **dry or barely-damp** microfibre cloth.
- **Never** use solvents, bleach or abrasive cleaners on screens or
  electronics.
- Don't let any liquid get into buttons, faders, vents or sockets.
- Clean when the system is **off** wherever possible.

---

## Microphones

Microphones are shared, so keep them hygienic.

| Microphone | How to clean |
|------------|--------------|
| Handheld (Ch 2, 3) | Wipe the body with a barely-damp cloth. The foam/grille can be wiped or, if removable, gently cleaned and **fully dried** before refitting. |
| Headset (Ch 4) | Wipe the earpiece/headband with a barely-damp cloth or an alcohol wipe on the parts that touch skin. Keep moisture away from the capsule. |
| Lectern / Pulpit (Ch 1, 5) | Gently dust the capsule; wipe the gooseneck/stand with a dry cloth. |
| Rode M5 pair | Dust only. These are delicate condenser mics — don't wet them. |

!!! warning "Let everything dry fully"
    Never put a damp microphone or windshield back into use. Moisture can
    damage the capsule and affect sound.

---

## Screens (TVs and monitors)

1. Turn the screen **off**.
2. Wipe gently with a **dry** microfibre cloth.
3. For marks, use a cloth **very slightly** dampened with water (never spray
   the screen).

Do **not** use glass cleaner or paper towel on TV screens — it can damage the
coating.

---

## Mixer, RodeCaster, StreamDeck and PC

- **Dust** with a dry cloth or a soft brush.
- Keep drinks **away** from the desk — a spill on the QU-5D or StreamDeck can
  be very costly.
- Do not vacuum directly over faders/buttons; brush dust away gently.

!!! danger "No drinks at the AV desk"
    The most common avoidable damage is a **spilled drink** on the mixer or
    computer. Keep all drinks well away from the equipment.

---

## Cameras

- Dust the camera **body** with a dry cloth.
- For the **lens**, use only a proper **lens cloth** or lens tissue, very
  gently. Never use household cleaners on a lens.
- Do not force the camera to move by hand — use the controller.

---

## Cables and connectors

- Keep cables **tidy** and unstrained.
- If a connector is dusty, a quick puff of air is enough — don't poke objects
  into sockets.

---

## What to leave to Mills IT

- Opening any equipment.
- Cleaning inside vents or fans.
- Removing fader knobs or panels.
- Anything that needs a tool.

➡️ Log anything that needs attention on [Regular Checks](regular-checks.md).
