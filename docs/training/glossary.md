# Glossary

Plain-English meanings of the words and equipment names used in this
documentation. If a term in another page is unfamiliar, look it up here.

---

## Equipment names

**Allen & Heath QU-5D**
: The audio **mixer** — the large desk with sliders. It balances all sound and
  creates the room, foyer and livestream mixes. See
  [QU-5D Mixer](../audio/qu5d-overview.md).

**JBL SRX812P**
: The **powered speakers** in the main auditorium. "Powered" means each speaker
  has its own amplifier built in.

**Foyer amplifier**
: The amplifier that drives the **foyer speakers**, fed by its own mix from the
  QU-5D.

**RodeCaster Video**
: The **video switcher and livestream encoder**. Combines the cameras with the
  livestream audio and streams to YouTube. See
  [RodeCaster Video](../video/rodecaster-video.md).

**Camera 1 — RoboShot HDMI 12**
: The first **PTZ camera**, usually the **wide** shot.

**Camera 2 — AVKANS 20X PTZ Camera Pro**
: The second **PTZ camera**, usually **close-ups**; has a 20× zoom.

**AVKANS Joy Pro Controller**
: The **joystick** box used to move the cameras by hand. See
  [PTZ Controller](../video/ptz-controller.md).

**StreamDeck XL**
: The panel of **labelled buttons** on the desk. Each button runs a job (switch
  camera, start stream, change slide). See
  [StreamDeck Buttons](../presentation/streamdeck-buttons.md).

**Bitfocus Companion**
: The **software** (on the NUC) that makes the StreamDeck buttons work.

**Presentation PC (NUC)**
: The small **Windows computer** that runs PowerPoint and Companion.

**Bluestream HDMI Matrix**
: The box that decides **which picture shows on which screen**. See
  [Bluestream Matrix](../displays/bluestream-matrix.md).

**Rode M5**
: A pair of **wired condenser microphones** used for the **choir and organ**.

**Sennheiser condenser microphone**
: The **wired** microphones at the **lectern** (Ch 1) and **pulpit** (Ch 5).

## Audio terms

**Mixer**
: The desk that combines and balances all the microphones and sends sound to
  the speakers, foyer and livestream.

**Fader**
: A **sliding** volume control for one source. Up = louder, down = quieter.

**Mute**
: A button that **silences** a channel even if its fader is up. Lit = silenced.

**Channel**
: One **input strip** on the mixer, e.g. "Channel 1 = lectern mic".

**Mix**
: A particular **balance** of sources for one destination. We have three: room,
  foyer and livestream.

**Matrix mix**
: A **separate, independently-balanced** mix. The foyer and livestream each use
  a matrix mix, which is why a mic can be in one mix but not another. See
  [Livestream Mix](../audio/livestream-mix.md).

**Main / LR fader**
: The fader controlling the **overall** level to the church speakers.

**Feedback**
: The loud **squeal** when an open mic hears the speakers and loops. Fix by
  lowering that mic's fader.

**Condenser microphone**
: A sensitive **wired** microphone (no battery needed) — our lectern, pulpit
  and Rode M5 mics.

**Radio (wireless) microphone**
: A **battery-powered** wireless microphone — our two handhelds and the
  headset.

## Video terms

**PTZ**
: **Pan, Tilt, Zoom** — a motorised camera that can turn and zoom on command.

**Pan**
: Turning the camera **left/right**.

**Tilt**
: Tipping the camera **up/down**.

**Zoom**
: Making the picture **closer (in)** or **wider (out)**.

**Preset**
: A **saved camera position** (pan/tilt/zoom) you can recall with one button.

**Switching**
: Choosing **which camera** the livestream shows. It does not move the cameras.

**Source**
: An input — e.g. a camera or the PC — that can be shown or sent somewhere.

**Encoder**
: A device that **packages** video for streaming. The RodeCaster Video does
  this for YouTube.

**Livestream**
: The live broadcast of the service to **YouTube**.

## Display terms

**HDMI**
: The common **cable/connector** that carries picture and sound between
  devices and TVs.

**Matrix**
: A switch that can route **any input to any output** — our Bluestream matrix
  sends the PC picture to the chosen screens.

**Confidence monitor**
: A screen facing the **speaker** so they can see the slides without turning
  around.

**Input / Output**
: An **input** is a source coming in (e.g. the PC). An **output** is a screen
  going out (e.g. Front TV Left).

**No Signal**
: A TV message meaning it is **not receiving a picture** — usually wrong input
  or routing. See [TV Not Working](../troubleshooting/tv-not-working.md).

## General terms

**Slide Show mode**
: PowerPoint showing slides **full screen** (press **F5**), with no menus.

**Scene** (QU-5D)
: A **saved snapshot** of all mixer settings — recall it to return to the
  normal Sunday setup.

**Power-cycle**
: To turn a device **off, wait, and on again** — a common fix.

**Mills IT**
: The company that **maintains** this system. Contact details on
  [Regular Checks](../maintenance/regular-checks.md#contacting-mills-it).
